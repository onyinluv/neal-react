Overview

P4PE Private Education Loan (PEL) Services

The P4PE Private Education Loan (PEL) services have been designed to provide the much needed funding professionals can use to advance their career. P4PE Institute is piloting its PEL services for the HR Professionals who want to undertake one of the international certifications.

Certification provides recognition of professional achievement, aids in your career advancement, serves as a visual reminder of your professional status, demonstrates a mastery of body of knowledge on an international level and a commitment to keeping abreast of new developments in your field, and provides you with evidence of broadbased knowledge, experience and competence.

Eligibility

Professionals assessing this facility must meet the following P4PE PEL criteria:

  • Must be currently working (or have a co-signer who is working)
  • Must be qualified for the certification exam
  • Must have registered for and accepted for the certification prep program (in-class, self-study or online study)

Notice to Applicants

You can significantly lower your loan requirement by paying more than the minimum 20% required initial upfront contribution

You can lower your monthly repayment value by extending the number of months or increasing your initial upfront contribution

You are strongly encouraged to pursue the availability of free or lower cost financial aid with your employerâ€™s professional development re-imbursement scheme where available

A loan request may be reduced or rejected depending on the applicant eligibility and employment status. We will certify different loans considering different data.

Applicants will be awarded the PEL facility up to the cost of the EFA but cannot be awarded more than the cumulative 50% of their take home pay for the loan duration. Loan duration and monthly repayment value may be adjusted to accommodate the amount requested.

The applicant EFA awarded is affected by their chosen certification eligibility and duration of employment with their current employer. Here is a case study to be used as a guide in understanding the PCL committee decision:

  • Less than or equal to 6 months employment => maximum 3 months repayment
  • Less than or equal to 12 months employment => maximum 6 months repayment
  • Above 12 months employment => up to 12 months repayment
  • Minimum certification eligibility => maximum 3 months repayment
  • Average certification eligibility => maximum 6 months repayment
  • High certification eligibility => up to 12 months repayment
